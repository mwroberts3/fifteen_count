Arcade

Arcade is the main mode in Indigo Loop, and individual games can last anywhere from about 2 to 20 minutes for experienced players. The are several nuances to this mode, the mastery and understanding of which can separate an average and good score.

Phases

There are two phases of play that make up the core of the gameplay loop in Arcade Mode. The Count! phase involves tapping cards and trying to get a total that adds up to 15. The Combo! phase immediately follows, and lets players sacrifice cards in order score points and shrink their hand.

Scoring Points

The best way to enjoy the Arcade mode is to play with only two general rules in mind:

  • card values must add up to 15 to submit
  • checking or comboing cards that are the same color results in more points

    • Pretty much every aspect of gameplay can result in more points. The bulk of points will generally be scored by checking cards in the Count! phase. Additional points can be scored in Combo! phase.

    Jackpot Cards

    During the course of a game, random cards will be selected as jackpot cards. Try to play the jackpot card when they appear to rack up bonus points, which are based on the total amount of cards played.

    Jackpot cards are indicated by a special golden border.

    Adding Time

    Through an adequate number of cards during the Count phase, players can add more time to the timer. The regular time bonus level is indicated by the bar of green blocks underneath the timer. Submitting cards during the Combo phase will replenish part of the time bonus.

    Restoring Cards

    Cards will be restored if player successfully checks and plays all of the cards in their hand, completing an indigo loop.

    Time Attack

    Time Attack is a less complex game mode than Arcade and is ideal for shorter play sessions, as rounds only last three minutes.

    The goal of Time Attack is to play as many cards as possible within the 180 second timer, and unlike Arcade mode there is no way to add more seconds to the clock. So all you have is 180 seconds. Make them count!

    Like Arcade mode, you'll want to click cards and have their values add up to 15. A key difference is that once 15 is reached, the cards are automatically submitted.

    Cards come in a mix of two colors, with values of -1 to 12. Assume that playing cards of the same color will result in more points.

    Additionally, there are two types of special cards. Bonus and Shuffle cards. The Bonus card starts in the first position at the start of each round. You should try to get the bonus card in the final position, by submitting 15 count pairs in between the start and end.

    A Bonus card played from the final position will result in maximum points.

    A Shuffle card appears on a random card, and if this card is played, your entire hand will be redealt and the Bonus card will be reset to the first position. These cards can sometimes be used strategically.
