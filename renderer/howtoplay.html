Arcade

Arcade mode games can last anywhere from 100 seconds to 20 minutes or more, depending on the players skill level and knowledge of the game.

Phases

There are two phases of play that make up the core of the gameplay loop in Arcade Mode. The Count! phase involves tapping cards and trying to get a total that adds up to 15. The Combo! phase immediately follows and lets players sacrifice cards in order score points and shrink their hand.

Scoring Points

Pretty much every aspect of gameplay can result in more points. The bulk of points will generally be scored by checking cards in the Count! phase. Additional points can be scored in Combo! phase. Successfully playing the jackpot can also result in large points gains.

Tip - the best way to enjoy the Arcade mode is to play with only two general rules in mind:
  • card values must add up to 15 to submit
  • checking or comboing cards that are the same color results in more points.

    • Adding Time

    Time management is paramount to having a successful run. Time can be added through particular acheivements: checking at least half the cards in a players hand or checking all cards in a players hand.

    Restoring Cards

    A player can reset to a fullhand of 10 cards if they successfully check all the cards in their hand, which can only happen if the respective card values of each card adds up to 15.

    Time Attack

    In Time Attack players have 180 seconds to score as many points as possible. They are presented a mix of 18 red and black cards with values of -1 to 12. Like Arcade mode the foundation of gameplay consists of players clicking on cards and adding up their numbers to 15.

    Unlike Arcade mode, players do not have to have to submit cards after a total of 15 is reached; the cards are automatically submitted.

    Types of Cards

    There are three types of cards in Time Attack. All types of cards are based on the Standard Card.

  • Standard Card - the standard card is either black or red
  • Bomb Card - as a Time Attack game progresses standard card become bomb cards. This is indicated by a bomb icon in the top corner of the card. If bomb cards are included as part of a 15 count sumbit. The the current cards are scrapped and replaced by an entirely new hand of 18 cards
  • Bonus Card - when a time attack game starts the card in the first position, (top left corner), is the designated bonus card. This is indicated by a colorful, animated border

    • Utilizing the Bonus Card

    Correctly using the bonus card can separate a decent Time Attack score and a great Time Attack Score. At the start of a game, the bonus card starts in the top left corner of the player's hand. As a player submits 15 counts from the center of the hand. The bonus card will make its way across and down the board as new cards replace the played ones.

    If the bonus card makes it to final position in the bottom right position of the player's hand, its score enhancing power is unlocked. You can now submit the bonus card in a regular submit to apply a points multiplier to the score of the cards just played.
