Arcade

Arcade is the primary mode of Indigo Loop. The goal of Arcade mode is to score as many points as possible before time runs out. To start, the player is presented with 10 cards of two differents colors and 60 seconds on the clock; more time can be added during play. There are multiple ways to score points, across two unique gameplay phases. Making plays with cards of the same color almost always results in more points.

Phases

There are two distinct gameplay phases in Arcade mode. The Count! and Combo! phases. The Combo! phase immediately follows the Count! phase. Points can be scored in either.

Count!

At the start of a game, players are presented with 10 cards in one of two colors with values ranging from -1 to 15. The player can click on any of the cards shown to count it. Multiple cards can be counted at any time and the total sum of their values is shown in the translucent, rounded container in the center of the bottom portion of the player’s interface.

The goal of the Count! phase is to count any number of cards whose values add up to 15. Once a 15 count is reached the player can submit the counted cards and move on to the Combo! phase.

The player also has the option to swap the last card in their hand for a new one. They can either press the swap button or click the button in the player’s interface. Time is subtracted from the countdown timer when a card is swapped, so use this option carefully.

Combo!

In the Combo! phase players can choose to sacrifice cards to rack up combo points. Beware though, a card that is combo'd will be removed from your hand, at least temporarily.

Strategically choosing when to combo card, and clicking them in the most rewarding sequence, will result in big point gains.

Card Types

There are three main types of cards: cards with a single value, compound cards with two values and cards with a -1 value. These values have to be carefully considered during the Count! phase, as the player's goal is to click on cards whose values add up to 15. There is also a modified card, the jackpot card.

Compound

Compound cards have two values, and allow the player choose which value to use. The player chooses the value by pressing the corresponding key or by clicking value displayed in the interface.

Values on multiple compound cards can be counted at once. Watch the short video to see a demonstration.

Jackpot

During the course of a game, random cards will be selected as jackpot cards. Try to count and play the jackpot card when it appears to rack up points and raise the jackpot level. The amount of points added depends on the current jackpot level and the total number of cards played up to that point.

Jackpot cards are indicated by a color changing border and a notification above the game board.

The jackpot level will change during the course of a game. Skilled players should be able to consistently keep it at a high level.

Adding Time and Cards

Half-Hand Bonus

Extra time can be added to the countdown timer if a player plays at least half of the cards in their hand during the Count! phase. Strategically counting cards to add extra time is essential to racking up good high scores.

The amount of time added depends on the current level of the bonus meter, which is shown underneath the countdown timer display. The bonus meter is replenished by sacrificing cards in the Combo! phase.

Indigo Loops

If a player successfully counts and plays all the cards in their hand, they’ll complete an Indigo Loop and all ten cards will be replenished. They will also get additional seconds added to the countdown timer.

In other words, if a player recognizes that the value of all the cards in their hand adds up to 15, they can play them all and replinish the previously sacrificed cards.

Time Attack

Time attack has a set time limit of 180 seconds. Unlike Arcade mode, there is no way to add more seconds to the timer. The player is presented with 18 cards of two colors and random values at the start of play. Like Arcade mode, the player needs to click cards to count them and have their values add up to 15. The cards are automatically submitted once a 15-count is reached.

Cards come in a mix of two colors, with values ranging from -1 to 12. Counting cards of the same color will result in more points. Additionally, there are two specialty cards: the Bonus and Shuffle cards.

Random cards will be selected as Shuffle cards each turn, indicated by an explosion icon in the top right corner. If this card is played, the player's entire hand will be redealt and the bonus card will be reset to the first position. The number of shuffle cards in play increases as time goes on.

The bonus card starts in the first, top left, position at the start of each round. The player should try to get the bonus card to the final position, bottom right, by submitting 15 count combos in between the card's current position and the final position.

the bonus card will spin in final position

Bonus cards played from the final position will result in a points windfall.. After a bonus card is played, it'll be reset to the first position. A player should try to submit as many bonus cards from the final position as they can. Subsequent plays with the bonus cards will result in more points. Shuffle cards played along with bonus card in final position, will not effect the points bonus.
